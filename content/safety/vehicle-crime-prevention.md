# Vehicle Crime Prevention

Vehicle break-ins remain the **number one crime issue in many Calgary suburbs**, including communities in the northwest. The encouraging part is that many incidents are preventable with a few consistent habits.

Calgary Police Service has reported that **more than 60% of vehicle thefts involve unlocked vehicles**. That is a reminder that quick, everyday actions matter.

## How to reduce the risk

- **Lock your doors** every time, even when parked in your driveway
- **Hide or remove valuables** so nothing is visible through the windows
- **Close garage doors** promptly and do not leave remote openers in unlocked vehicles
- Park in a well-lit area whenever possible
- Avoid leaving wallets, backpacks, work gear, sunglasses, and charging cables in plain sight
- Check that your vehicle is actually locked before heading inside for the night

## Watch for suspicious patterns

Please report **overnight parking of unfamiliar vehicles** when the behaviour seems unusual or repeated, especially if people appear to be checking doors or moving slowly through the neighbourhood.

Contact Calgary Police Service at **403-266-1234** for non-emergency concerns. If a crime is in progress, call **911**.

## A strong neighbourhood habit

Vehicle crime prevention works best when the whole community stays alert. A quick glance outside, a reminder to a neighbour, or a timely report can help stop repeat incidents before they spread from one block to the next.
