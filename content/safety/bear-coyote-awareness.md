# Bear and Coyote Awareness

Rocky Ridge and Royal Oak sit right on Calgary's northwest edge, next to open ranch land and close to Bearspaw. That means wildlife sightings are a real part of life here. Residents regularly see **bears, coyotes, and deer** along pathways, near storm ponds, and around green spaces.

Seeing wildlife can be a special part of living here, but it also means we need to be thoughtful neighbours, both to each other and to the animals moving through the area.

## Reduce attractants at home

- Put out **garbage and compost only on collection day**
- Keep bin lids closed and clean up spills promptly
- Bring in **bird feeders** during bear season or if wildlife activity increases nearby
- Do not leave pet food, fallen fruit, or food waste outdoors
- Keep barbecue areas clean and free of grease build-up

## Protect pets and children

- Keep dogs **on leash** near pathways, wetlands, ravines, and natural areas
- Supervise small children when wildlife has been spotted nearby
- Keep cats indoors, especially overnight
- Give wildlife lots of space and never approach for a photo

## If you see a bear

- Stay calm and back away slowly
- Keep children close
- Do not run
- Give the bear a clear escape route
- Call **311**, **911**, or Alberta wildlife authorities if the bear is lingering in a residential area or behaving aggressively

## If you see a coyote

- Keep pets close and pick up small dogs if needed
- Make yourself look big, be loud, and do not feed or approach the animal
- Leave the area calmly
- Report concerning or repeated behaviour to the City

## Helpful resources

- [Alberta BearSmart](https://www.alberta.ca/bearsmart.aspx)
- [City of Calgary wildlife safety resources](https://www.calgary.ca/parks/wildlife.html)
- [Report a non-emergency concern to RRROCA](/safety/report/)

Wildlife awareness is part of living in a community that borders open land and natural habitat. A few smart habits help keep people, pets, and wildlife safer all season long.
