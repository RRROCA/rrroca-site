## Motion Text

**BE IT RESOLVED THAT** the Board of Directors of the Rocky Ridge Royal Oak Community Association adopt the *Integrated Technology Strategy* (dated May 2026) as the governing framework for technology decisions through the 2024–2028 strategic plan period.

## What This Motion Approves

1. **The strategy document itself** — 11 technology domains evaluated against 8 Architecture Principles derived from RRROCA's own Strategic Plan and Business Plan
2. **The "Frontier Community Association" vision** — RRROCA leverages modern, zero-cost technology to operate more effectively than associations ten times its size
3. **The governance framework** — which decisions require board motions vs. portfolio authority (Safety & Technology Director)
4. **The 5-phase implementation roadmap** — Foundation → Launch → Meeting Intelligence → Engagement → Hardening

## What This Motion Does NOT Approve

- **Specific platform decisions** (e.g., website hosting, newsletter tool) — these come as separate motions or portfolio-authority decisions under the strategy framework
- **Any spending** — the strategy is designed around zero-cost tools; any future expenditure would require its own motion
- **Binding commitments** — the strategy is a living document, updated as needs change

## Why This Comes First

Every technology decision the board makes — website platform, membership tools, communication channels — should be evaluated against a consistent set of principles. Without an adopted strategy:

- Each decision is made in isolation with no framework to evaluate trade-offs
- There's no shared vocabulary for discussing technology choices (e.g., "Does this survive volunteer turnover?")
- The Safety & Technology Director has no mandate to act on operational items

With an adopted strategy:

- The board has already agreed on *what matters* (the 8 principles)
- Individual decisions are faster because the evaluation criteria are pre-established
- Operational improvements (newsletter, cybersecurity, social media) can proceed under portfolio authority without consuming board meeting time

## Financial Impact

**$0.** This motion adopts a decision-making framework. All platform decisions with cost implications require separate approval.

The strategy itself projects **$3,100+ in savings** (eliminating WordPress hosting and the budgeted Tallis Design website overhaul) and **$3,000/year in potential volunteer matching revenue** — but those outcomes depend on subsequent platform decisions, not this motion.
