## Your Board, in the Open

Starting today, every formal decision the RRROCA Board of Directors makes is published right here on our website — in real time.

When a board member proposes a motion, it appears on the [Governance page](/board/) immediately. You can read exactly what's being proposed, who proposed it, how much it costs, and when the vote closes. When the board votes, the results are recorded permanently.

No more wondering what happened at a meeting you couldn't attend. No more waiting for meeting minutes that arrive weeks later. The decisions that affect your neighbourhood are now transparent, accessible, and on the record.

## What You Can See

- **Active motions** — what the board is voting on right now
- **Vote counts** — how many directors voted for, against, or abstained
- **Full motion text** — the complete proposal with rationale, costs, and details
- **Decision history** — every past motion and its outcome, permanently archived

## Why This Matters

Community associations work best when residents trust that decisions are made thoughtfully and transparently. Open governance means:

- **Accountability** — every vote is attributed, not anonymous
- **Participation** — residents can provide informed feedback on proposals
- **Trust** — you don't have to take anyone's word for what the board decided
- **Institutional memory** — decisions and their context are preserved, not lost when board members rotate

## See It in Action

Head to the [Governance page](/board/) to see active motions and the full decision archive. Have questions about a motion? Reach out to any board member — their contact information is on the [About page](/about/).

This is what a modern community association looks like. Welcome to transparent governance.
