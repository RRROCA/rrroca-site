# Safety Director Update: Spring 2026

As we head into warmer weather, I want to thank residents for helping keep Rocky Ridge and Royal Oak safe, watchful, and welcoming. Our local crime statistics remain **well below the Calgary average**, and that is good news for families across both neighbourhoods.

Spring and summer also tend to bring a few predictable issues, especially vehicle crime, open garages, and increased wildlife activity along our pathways and green spaces. A few simple habits can go a long way.

## Seasonal reminders

- **Lock your vehicles every night**, even if they are parked in the driveway.
- **Secure your garage** and avoid leaving the overhead door open when no one is outside.
- **Remove valuables from view**, including bags, chargers, sunglasses, and garage door openers.
- **Stay alert around bears and coyotes**, especially near pathways, natural areas, and the community edge bordering ranch land.
- **Report suspicious activity** as soon as possible, whether it is repeated overnight parking, prowling, or unusual behaviour around homes, garages, or vehicles.

## New online reporting form

RRROCA now has a dedicated [Safety Hub](/safety/) with resources for non-emergency concerns. For police matters, contact CPS non-emergency at [403-266-1234](tel:4032661234). For city issues, use [Calgary 311](https://311.calgary.ca). For neighbourhood awareness, join our [Block Watch Facebook group](https://www.facebook.com/groups/raborrroca) to share tips with neighbours.

Please remember: if something is urgent or an emergency, call **911** right away. For Calgary Police Service non-emergency matters, call **403-266-1234**.

## Wildlife awareness matters here

Living in northwest Calgary means sharing space with wildlife. Bears, coyotes, and deer are common in foothill-edge communities like ours, especially in spring and early summer. If you have not already, please review our [bear and coyote awareness page](/safety/bear-coyote-awareness/).

Thank you for watching out for your neighbours and for continuing to make community safety a shared effort.
