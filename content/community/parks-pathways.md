# Parks and Pathways

One of the best things about living in Rocky Ridge and Royal Oak is how easy it is to get outside. Our neighbourhoods are stitched together by parks, playgrounds, wetlands, and pathways that make everyday walks feel a little more scenic.

## Rocky Ridge: room to roam

Rocky Ridge has excellent pathway connections that lead residents toward **Bearspaw** and the remarkable **Twelve Mile Coulee** area. Whether you are out for a quick dog walk, a weekend bike ride, or an early-morning run with mountain views in the distance, there is always a route worth exploring.

In winter, many of these pathways remain favourites for walking, and on the right day they are also ideal for **cross-country skiing** close to home.

## Royal Oak: parks for play and gathering

Royal Oak offers a wonderful mix of family-friendly outdoor spaces, including **The Cascades** water feature, multiple playgrounds, and the community park that helps bring neighbours together through every season.

These spaces are used for everything from after-school meetups and stroller walks to summer playdates and community events.

## Four-season outdoor living

Across both neighbourhoods, the pathway network supports:

- Walking and running
- Family bike rides
- Dog walks on leash
- Nature viewing near ponds and open spaces
- Winter walks and cross-country skiing when conditions allow

If you are new to the area, start with a short walk and let the pathways guide you. If you have lived here for years, you already know how much these outdoor connections shape daily life in Rocky Ridge and Royal Oak.
