## Our Tiny Forest

RRROCA is proud to partner with the [Calgary Climate Hub](https://calgaryclimatehub.ca/) on a **Tiny Forest** initiative — a dense planting of native trees and shrubs using the Miyawaki method, designed to create a thriving micro-ecosystem right in our neighbourhood.

### What is a Tiny Forest?

A Tiny Forest is a dense, biodiverse planting of native species in a small urban area. Inspired by Japanese botanist Dr. Akira Miyawaki, these forests:

- **Grow 10× faster** than conventional plantings
- **Support 100× more biodiversity** than monoculture landscapes
- **Absorb more CO₂** per square metre than traditional urban trees
- **Create habitat** for pollinators, birds, and small wildlife
- **Cool the neighbourhood** through natural shade and evapotranspiration

### Community Impact

Our Tiny Forest brings hands-on climate action to Rocky Ridge and Royal Oak. Volunteers from the community — including families, school groups, and neighbours — came together to plant hundreds of native trees and shrubs.

The forest serves as:

- A **living classroom** for local schools
- A **pollinator sanctuary** supporting bees, butterflies, and birds
- A **neighbourhood gathering point** that connects residents to nature
- A tangible example of **community-led climate resilience**

### About the Calgary Climate Hub

The Calgary Climate Hub is a local non-profit driving community-level climate action through projects like Tiny Forests, community gardens, and sustainability education. Their partnership with RRROCA demonstrates what neighbours can accomplish when they work together for a greener future.
